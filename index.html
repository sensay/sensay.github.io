We are better together.

It seems every day, another startup or ICO is launched which aims to give users access and control of their data. This is usually done by means of a token, a dApp or an application. However, this means that many companies are building centralized versions of databases masked as decentralized systems.

We have to stop the creation of new silos of data, which are not shared between interested actors. We want people to easily use and shift their data between services; such as shifting identity data in Civic over to SelfKey, or their knowledge attribution data in SENSE to Datum. 

This is what the Blockchain Data Alliance aims to help with. It is an open community standard which aims to allow any website or user to do interact programmatically with identity and persona services. It also allows a user to view and control their information and give permission to services to access information. Ideally, this can be done using the websites, dApps or tokens that power them. Join the movement to grow and shape these standards.

Fix missing data links

Data connection is still broken. Decentralization aims to fix this fundamental flaw of centralized databases in silos. Creating links with common standards is the solution.

Empower data creators

The user truly owns their data. It's as hard for data authors and content creators to unlock data as it is to access health records. Access must be granted on a permission basis.

Reduce redundancy

How many times have you entered your resume on job websites or your fill out your profile, email address and phone number into forms? Standardizing keeps data fresh and users synced.

Standardize a global protocol

The Internet operates on some standards that help move content fluidly, yet underlying data is largely centralized. RSS, JSON, HTML and others have started the mission.
